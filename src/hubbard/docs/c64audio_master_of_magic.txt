C64Audio.com "Master of Magic" Blog Series
============================================
Source: https://c64audio.com/blogs/rob-hubbard-master-of-magic
Author: Chris Abbott
Retrieved: 2026-04-11

NOTE: "Master of Magic" is a full book by Chris Abbott containing "6502 assembly-
language music driver excerpts from Rob Hubbard's original code." The blog posts are
promotional excerpts that are primarily historical/biographical, NOT the technical
driver code excerpts. The book itself would be the source for those.

Book: "Master of Magic - The Official Rob Hubbard Softography"
Available at: https://c64audio.com/products/master-of-magic-softography

BLOG POST 1: "Rob Hubbard - The Beginning" (March 26, 2024)
=============================================================
Early biography. Hubbard taught himself 6510 assembly in 1983.
Began with unsuccessful October 1984 mailshot to game companies.
His breakthrough came through Mastertronic (Action Biker) and
Incentive (Confuzion).

BLOG POST 2: "Rob Hubbard - 1985 on the run" (July 8, 2024)
==============================================================
Prolific 1985 output chronicle.

Key technical points:
- Jason Perkins of Micro Projects found Hubbard's mailshot, leading to
  "Thing on a Spring" which got ZZAP! 64's highest-ever music rating
- Zoids and Master of Magic drew inspiration from Synergy's album "Audion"
  (Larry Fast) — "turned Rob from a video game musician into a genuine
  soundtrack composer"
- By March 1986, Hubbard had completed all work using his "classic" driver

BLOG POST 3: "The High Cs - Comets, Commando, Chimera" (September 3, 2024)
============================================================================
Historical context for July/August 1985 compositions.

- Crazy Comets: Instrument-switching techniques perfected in "Gerry the Germ"
- Commando: Created under time pressure for PCW show in September
- Chimera: Fretless bass sound, mined from pre-SID back catalog
- "Chimera benefitted from the creative and sonic development that Rob had done
  with Zoids and Master of Magic"

No technical driver details in this post.

BLOG POST 4: "Rob Hubbard Thrusts into 1986" (July 13, 2024)
==============================================================
KEY TECHNICAL CONTENT about the driver evolution:

- "Hollywood or Bust" = LAST tune done in the "classic" driver
- Atari 8-bit work: Hubbard ported his code to Atari, exploiting the POKEY chip's
  "distortion" feature. Lessons learned fed back into the experimental C64 driver.
- "W.A.R." game prompted creation of "a much more serious-sounding driver"
- NEW DRIVER programmed in April 1986 (first released September 1987)
- Sound was "a world away from the accessible bounciness" of earlier work
- Sanxion loading theme = "another major milestone... something that simply
  couldn't have been done in the old driver"
- Z80 work: David Whittaker swap arrangement (Whittaker got Atari support,
  Hubbard got Z80 drivers)
- 1986 represented "transformation rather than continuation"
