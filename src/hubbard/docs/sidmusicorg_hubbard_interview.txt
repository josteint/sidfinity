Interviews with Rob Hubbard (sidmusic.org)
Includes: Commodore Zone interview (Waz and Kenz), and Happy Computer issue 7/86 interview.

=== COMMODORE ZONE INTERVIEW (circa 1997-1998) ===

--- TECHNICAL CONTENT ---

Kenz: How did you go about composing your C64 tunes? Did you have a music editor
that you used, as there were often rumours you used one you made yourself?
Rob: No, I just used to an assembler and edit the source code - most
people in those days did the same. There really wasn't time to
sit and write an editor, as there was so much work to do.

Kenz: Are there any unusual stories behind any of your C64 tunes?
Rob: Thing On A Spring was actually something I wrote to test my first driver.

Waz: The music from "I-Ball" was inspired by Cabaret Voltaire...
Rob: Looking back I don't think the tune really worked very well, and some of
what I was trying to do was pushing the driver too much.

Kenz: Why were all those music jingles hidden in "Delta"? Was the idea to play a
jingle when you originally collected a power-up?
Rob: These are all small clips from Sanxion and Romeo/Juliet music.
They were all supposed to be for short stingers such as end of level,
extra life etc....

=== HAPPY COMPUTER ISSUE 7/86 INTERVIEW (1986) ===

--- TECHNICAL CONTENT ---

Happy: How long does it take you to create a song?
Rob: Normally it takes about two weeks to compose and arrange. In some special
cases where I had to do it faster, I managed to do one in two days. That's how
"Commando", "Zoids" and "One Man and his Droid" were created.

Happy: Do you use programs like "Music Studio"?
Rob: No. I do my music in pure machine code using an assembler. I tested those
programs, but their possibilities are limited. "Electrosound" is the only one
that I like.

NOTE: "Electrosound" is the only third-party music program Hubbard found useful.
All composition was done directly in machine code / assembler.
