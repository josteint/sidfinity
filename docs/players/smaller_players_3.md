# Smaller Players - Batch 3

## Ariston (148 tunes)

- **Author:** Ian Crabtree (driver, 1987), Philip Brabbin (GUI editor)
- **Type:** Commercial game music driver (UK-based)
- **Source:** Not public
- **CSDb:** #29914
- **Notes:** Most composers (Allister Brimble, Barry Leitch, Jonathan Dunn, Matt Gray, Wally Beben) arranged directly in assembler source. Multiple versions tuned at 424 or 434 Hz. Ported to Atari ST and Amiga in 1988.

## MoN/Deenen (140 tunes)

- **Author:** Charles Deenen of Maniacs of Noise (founded 1987)
- **Type:** Commercial game music driver
- **Source:** Not public
- **Notes:** Driver called "Musicfile." Composers typed hex numbers and labels into assembler source — no GUI. Two versions: MoN Old (1989-90), MoN New (1990-92). After group split, Jeroen Tel wrote his own driver. Also ported to ZX Spectrum 128K.

## Reflextracker (137 tunes)

- **Author:** Tammo Hinrichs (kb), Matthias Kramm (Quiss), Zorc, PVCF — from Reflex/The Obsessed Maniacs
- **Year:** 1995
- **Type:** PC-based tracker targeting C64 SID
- **Source:** Not public
- **CSDb:** #43348
- **Notes:** Supported QuadSID for up to 10 channels. Standard C64 output = 3 channels. QuadSID compositions required manual remixing for 3-channel SID.

## LordsOfSonics/MS (134 tunes)

- **Author:** Markus Schneider (co-founded Lords of Sonics with Jens Blidon, 1988)
- **Type:** Game music driver (German scene)
- **Source:** Not public
- **Notes:** Driver evolved into Parsec Music Editor then Compotech. Schneider joined X-Ample Architectures in 1989. Used on Rolling Ronny, No Mercy, Lethal Zone, Xiphoids.

## Prosonix (131 tunes)

- **Author:** Stein Pedersen (Zizyphus) of Offence/Panoramic Designs/Prosonix (Oslo, Norway)
- **Year:** 1988
- **Type:** Native C64 editor
- **Source:** Not public
- **CSDb:** #179618
- **Notes:** Multiple variants: original, "new", "tiny". Limited documentation.

## Vibrants/JO (130 tunes)

- **Author:** Poul-Jesper Olsen (JO) of Vibrants (Denmark)
- **Type:** Custom player
- **Source:** Not public
- **Notes:** Professional game audio (Interactivision, Brain Bug, FunSoft). Also coded AdLib, Amiga, and MT-32 players. Separate from Vibrants/Laxity (different player, same group).

## CyberTracker (128 + 130 exe variant = 258 total tunes)

- **Author:** Bjarke Norgaard Laustsen (CyberBrain) of No Name
- **Year:** 2001
- **Documentation:** Good — online manual at noname.c64.org/tracker/
- **Source:** Not public
- **CSDb:** #2601
- **Notes:** Native C64 tracker. Patterns 0-128 lines, max 796 pattern lines, 512 track lines, 768 envelope points, 256 patterns, 31 instruments. Instruments: 8 graphical envelopes (volume/ADSR, waveform, pulse width, filter passband, cutoff, resonance, pitch mod, pitch mode). Supports vibrato and arpeggio. V1.00/V1.01 format compatible.

## SidWinder (117 tunes)

- **Author:** Balazs Takacs (Taki) of Natural Beat
- **Year:** 1999 (V1.22)
- **Source:** V1.23 source reportedly in C64 FTP archives
- **CSDb:** #66494
- **Notes:** Native C64 editor. Limited documentation.

## SynC (108 tunes)

- **Author:** Matthias Hartung (The Syndrom) of Crest/Pretzel Logic/The Imperium Arts
- **Full name:** SYNdrom Composer
- **Source:** Not public
- **CSDb:** #7850
- **Notes:** Earlier versions called "64'er Music Editor" / "64'er Musikeditor."

## DefMon (107 tunes)

- **Author:** Mats (Frantic) of Hack'n'Trade
- **Year:** 2008 (first public release 2013, active through 2020+)
- **Documentation:** Good — wiki at defmon.vandervecken.com
- **Source:** Not public (but defMONRelocator tool on GitHub: github.com/dkt64/defMONRelocator)
- **CSDb:** #196474
- **Notes:** Native C64 tracker. S64 format. 32 instruments, 1x-8x speed, programmable filter. MIDI sync, DIN-sync, Gameboy sync (LSDj/Nanoloop), modular synth sync. Stereo SID support. Can pack as executables.

## System6581 (104 tunes)

- **Author:** Fredrik Hederstierna (Zizyphus) of Oneway
- **Year:** 1989-1990 (released 1993, V3.0)
- **Source:** Not public
- **CSDb:** #27434
- **Notes:** Native C64 editor. Included 12 demo tracks.

## NinjaTracker V2.x (97 tunes)

- **Author:** Lasse Oorni (Cadaver) of Covert Bitops (same author as GoatTracker)
- **Year:** 2002+
- **Documentation:** Good
- **Source:** Yes — player source (nt2play.s) included with distribution. GitHub fork: github.com/localhost/NinjaTracker
- **CSDb:** #7206
- **Notes:** Minimal native C64 tracker designed for low memory/rastertime (smaller than GoatTracker). Up to 16 subtunes. Data: track data (patterns, transpose, loops), pattern data (note/keyoff/keyon, command, duration 3-65), tables (wave, pulse, filter). Note range C-1 to B-7. Commands double as instruments. GT2NT2 converter exists for GoatTracker2 songs.
